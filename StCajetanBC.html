Saint Cajetan Band Club

The St. Cajetan Band Club was founded in 1906 when some band members that played with the St. Joseph’s Band (1889) decided to not attend for a concert that band was going to do during the feast in Floriana. After this, these members were brought before the band’s committee and asked to give a reason for their behaviour. ...

The committee decided that the excuse brought from the members was not a valid one and as punishment they decided to remove him from the club. Some other members thought that this was not just and decided to join him in founding a new club and competitor. They founded the new band club on the 24th of June 1906, Feast of St. John the Baptist and named their new club after the patron saint of Hamrun, St. Cajetan.

Francesco Buttigieg was the mind behind the new club and is considered to be the founder. Immediately, Buttigieg and his friends started looking for a new place from where the club could function. They found a small dwelling in St. Joseph’s High Road, numbered 707. Later, from there the club moved to 699 in the same street, after which they moved to another place, where today once can find the LEGA. Finally, the club moved to its current location, Palazzo Thiene in St. Joseph High Road, a few meters away from the parish church.

The St. Cajetan Band performed for the first time during the feast of St. Cajetan on the 6th and 7th of August 1906, exactly 44 days after its foundation. The club was given the nickname ‘Tat-Tamal’ (dates). This nickname came from the supporters of the other band club who started calling their rivals this because next door to the club’s sede was a small shop that baked and sold dates.

The main aim of the club was to cultivate a culture of music amongst the locals, this encouraged members to start a music school in the late eighties. This school is still open to this day. Anyone can go and start learning music, the club even offers its learners a free instrument when learning. The club is the proud owner of a beautiful collection of music, from marches, funeral marches, classical pieces and more, the club has a variety of music scores in its archives. Undoubtedly, some of the most valuable music the club owns are the ones that were written by its conductors. One can mention the Clubs Hymn, a beautiful composition of Mro. Joseph Abela Scolaro with the lyrics written by Fr Frans Camilleri.

One of the hardest working sub-committees of the club is the youth committee. Founded in 1987, the youth committee was always one the club valued the most. Youths within the club work voluntarily to help the club function, prosper and grow, organising fund-raising activities to help sustain the club.
